About Me

My name is Austin Le, and I am a 4th year undergraduate student at UC Berkeley pursuing a B.S. in Electrical Engineering & Computer Science (EECS). As a student in the EECS Honors Degree program, I am also pursuing an additional breadth within Cognitive Science and Psychology.

I love learning, teaching, experimenting with technology, contemplating the real and virtual worlds, and immersing myself in video games.

On campus, I am currently the President of Eta Kappa Nu, Mu chapter (the EECS Honor Society), serving as an Undergraduate Student Instructor for CS 184, Computer Graphics and Imaging, and working as an undergraduate researcher in the Visual Computing Lab working with graduate student Ben Mildenhall under Professor Ren Ng.

In an academic setting, I enjoy studying both the theoretical and practical sides of computer science. However, my main interests lie in the applications of computer graphics, image processing, computational imaging, and human-computer interaction that create interesting and powerful technology that augments everyday life for people. Particular applications I am fascinated by are virtual reality, augmented reality, and real-time rendering. I'm a virtual and augmented reality enthusiast and hope to become more and more involved in its development as I continue to pursue my professional career. With my breadth in Cognitive Science and Psychology, I hope to apply concepts in human perception and cognition to research and development being done in these areas.

Outside of school, I am working on improving my web development skills in order to slowly improve the presentation and quality of my websites and project reports. I am also very passionate about online multiplayer video games and greatly enjoy what they have to offer: immersive worlds, challenging experiences, and team-oriented, strategic gameplay. I play a large variety of games, but some notable favorites of mine over the years have been RuneScape, League of Legends, and both the original Guild Wars 1 and its sequel Guild Wars 2.

In Summer 2014, I interned at Google's Mountain View office on the Feedback team, and in Summer 2015, I returned to work at Google's Seattle office on the App Engine Admin API team (part of Google Cloud Platform). In Summer 2016, I was a Software Engineering Intern at Riot Games in Los Angeles, working within their Service Availability Initiative, on the rCluster team, which develops and builds back-end infrastructure and systems that help run League of Legends at a global scale.
