About Me

My name is Austin Le, and I am a 4th year undergraduate student at UC Berkeley pursuing a B.S. in Electrical Engineering & Computer Science (EECS). As a student in the EECS Honors Degree program, I am also pursuing an additional breadth within Cognitive Science and Psychology.

I love learning, teaching, experimenting with technology, contemplating the real and virtual worlds, and immersing myself in video games.

On campus, I am currently the President of Eta Kappa Nu, Mu chapter (the EECS Honor Society), an Undergraduate Student Instructor for CS 184 (Computer Graphics and Imaging), and an undergraduate researcher in the Visual Computing Lab working with graduate student Ben Mildenhall under Professor Ren Ng.

My academic and research interests are in computer graphics, computer vision, human-computer interaction, virtual reality, and augmented reality. With my breadth in Cognitive Science and Psychology, I hope to apply concepts in human perception and cognition to research and development in these fields and be a part of the next revolution in graphics and VR/AR.

In my free time, I have been improving my web development skills, reading, and attempting to maintain a regular gym schedule. I am also passionate online gamer and greatly enjoy what modern video games have to offer: immersive worlds, challenging experiences, and team-oriented, strategic gameplay. I play a large variety of games, but some notable favorites of mine over the years have been RuneScape, League of Legends, and both the original Guild Wars and its sequel Guild Wars 2.
