About Me

My name is Austin Le, and I am a 4th year undergraduate student at UC Berkeley pursuing a B.S. in Electrical Engineering & Computer Science (EECS). As a student in the EECS Honors Degree program, I am also pursuing an additional breadth within Cognitive Science and Psychology.

I love learning, teaching, experimenting with technology, contemplating the real and virtual worlds, and immersing myself in video games.

On campus, I am currently involved in multiple student groups and activities, which include:

  • Department Relations Officer of Eta Kappa Nu, Mu chapter (the EECS Honor Society)
  • Undergraduate researcher in the Visual Computing Lab working with graduate student Ben Mildenhall under Professor Ren Ng
  • A research member in the ISAACS (Immersive Semi-Autonomous Aerial Command System) team as part of the Virtual Reality at Berkeley club working in conjunction with the Berkeley Robotics and Intelligent Machines Lab

In an academic setting, I enjoy studying both the theoretical and practical sides of computer science. However, my main interests lie in the applications of computer graphics, image processing, human-computer interaction, machine learning, and computer vision that help create interesting and powerful technology that augments everyday life for people. Particular applications I am fascinated by are virtual reality, augmented reality, context-aware computing, smart devices, and the Internet of Things. I'm a virtual and augmented reality enthusiast and hope to become more and more involved in its development as I continue to pursue my professional career. With my breadth in Cognitive Science and Psychology, I hope to apply concepts in human perception and cognition to research and development being done in virtual and augmented reality.

Outside of school, I am working on improving my web development skills in order to slowly improve the presentation and quality of my website. I am also very passionate about online multiplayer video games and greatly enjoy what they have to offer: immersive worlds, challenging experiences, and team-oriented, strategic gameplay. I play a large variety of games, but currently mostly play League of Legends, Guild Wars 2, and Dungeon Fighter Online. If I had to pick a specific genre of games I enjoy the most, it would be RPG (Role-Playing Games). I also enjoy experimenting with virtual reality games and applications using the HTC Vive and Unreal Engine 4.

In Summer 2014, I interned at Google's Mountain View office on the Feedback team, and in Summer 2015, I returned to work at Google's Seattle office on the App Engine Admin API team (part of Google Cloud Platform). In Summer 2016, I was a Software Engineering Intern at Riot Games in Los Angeles, working within their Service Availability Initiative, on the rCluster team, which develops and builds back-end infrastructure and systems that help run League of Legends at a global scale.
